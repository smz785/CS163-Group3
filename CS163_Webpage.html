Project Overview

This project explores uplift modeling — a technique for estimating the incremental impact of a treatment (such as an advertisement or promotion) on individual user behavior. Rather than simply predicting whether a user will convert, uplift modeling identifies who is most likely to convert because of the treatment.

Using the Criteo Uplift dataset, we perform exploratory data analysis, build predictive models, and visualize conversion lift across user segments. Our goal is to understand which users respond best to advertising and quantify the causal effect of treatment.

Research Question: Can we reliably estimate individual treatment effect (ITE) from observational advertising data, and which user features are most predictive of uplift?

Dataset

We use the Criteo Uplift Modeling Dataset v2.1, a large-scale benchmark dataset released by Criteo for causal inference and uplift research. It contains roughly 13 million rows of randomized controlled trial data from real advertising campaigns.

Methods & Pipeline

Our analysis follows a standard data science workflow:

  1. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) — Distribution of features, class balance of treatment and conversion, correlation analysis.
  2. Data Preprocessing — Handling missing values, normalization, train/test splitting while preserving treatment balance.
  3. Uplift Model Training — Two-model approach (separate models for treatment and control groups) and meta-learners (S-learner, T-learner).
  4. Evaluation — Uplift curve, Qini coefficient, and area under the uplift curve (AUUC) as primary metrics.
  5. Visualization & Deployment — Interactive Dash dashboard deployed on Google App Engine for exploration of model outputs.
Full EDA and modeling code is available in EDA.ipynb in our project repository.
